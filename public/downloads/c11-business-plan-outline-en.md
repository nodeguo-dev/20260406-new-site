# Business Plan Outline — C11 / Significant Benefit Work Permit (Sample)

> **Disclaimer:** This is a generic structural sample only, not legal advice. Tailor to your facts and IRCC guidance.

## 1. Executive Summary (1 page)

- Legal name, structure, principal business activity, location (city/province)  
- Your intended role (owner-operator, executive, key personnel)  
- 3–5 concrete bullets on **significant benefit** to Canada (preferably measurable)

## 2. Corporate & Ownership

- Incorporation / registration, BN, address  
- Shareholding and your decision-making authority  
- Related entities outside Canada (if any) and relationship to the Canadian entity

## 3. Product / Service & Market

- Problem solved, target customers, differentiation  
- Market size and go-to-market channels (realistic, verifiable)

## 4. Operations Plan (12–24 months)

- Milestones: hiring, launch, revenue/partnership targets  
- Key suppliers / partners  
- Regulatory compliance: licences, tax, employment (if applicable)

## 5. Benefit to Canada (core argument)

Use **specific, evidence-backed** bullets, e.g.:

- **Jobs:** roles, timeline, duties, compensation range (realistic)  
- **Economic:** investment deployed, conservative revenue/tax assumptions with stated caveats  
- **Industry/community:** skills gap addressed, technology/know-how, supply chain or community impact  

Avoid vague claims; tie each point to documents you can submit.

## 6. Financial Overview

- Source and availability of start-up capital  
- Simple 24-month forecast: revenue, major expenses, cash-flow assumptions  
- Key risks and mitigations

## 7. Your Qualifications & Fit

- Education and experience directly tied to the business  
- Why your **physical presence** in Canada is needed (vs. remote)

## 8. Annexes (as applicable)

- Corporate documents, bank statements summary, lease/LOI, CV, credentials, market excerpts, support letters, etc.
